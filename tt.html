Tech Talk-X

The objective of TechTalk-X is to provide a platform for undergraduate and postgraduate students to explore and present innovative ideas, research findings, and advancements in various technical fields.



Date and Time

March 21, 2024 (Thursday) & 01:00 PM Onwards




Location

Q-Block, Mandala Hall, Manav Rachna University, Sector – 43, Aravalli Hills, Delhi - Surajkund Road, Faridabad – 121004, (Haryana), India


Faculty Coordinator

Dr. Anjali Gautam

Ms. Sunita Joshi

Student Coordinator

Mr. Prince Yadav

Ms. Shweta


Eligibility

1. The event is open to undergraduate and postgraduate students from accredited educational institutions.
2. Participants must be enrolled in a recognized undergraduate or postgraduate program at the time of registration.

Registration

1. Participants must register for the event within the designated registration time frame.
2. Registration forms must be completed accurately and submitted via the specified platform.
3. The designated team size for this event is one individual.
4. Participants are required to carry their original valid college identity card.
5. Late registrations will not be accepted.

Presentation Topics

1. Presentations must focus on technical subjects related to exploration in any field of technology.
2. Topics may include, but are not limited to, emerging technologies, innovative solutions, research findings, and practical applications.
3. Presentations must be original work of the participants.
4. Non-technical presentations will be out rightly rejected.
5. No political or religious demonstrations should be done.

Presentation Format

1. Each presentation can be in the form of a PowerPoint or similar visual aid or can also be short powerful talk with focus on brevity and clarity.
2. Presentation/talk must be concise and well-structured, with a duration of 10 minutes extendable up to a maximum of 15 minutes.
3. Visual aids such as diagrams, charts, and videos may be used to enhance the presentation.
4. All presentations must be delivered in English only.

Judging Criteria

1. Presentations will be judged based on content and analytical ability, clarity & originality of work, relevance, technical skills and applications, and presentation skills.
2. Judges' decisions are final and binding.

Conduct

1. Participants must conduct themselves in a professional manner throughout the event.
2. Any form of plagiarism or academic dishonesty will result in immediate disqualification.
3. Participants must adhere to the event schedule and should reach the venue 20 minutes prior to the commencement of the event.

Awards

1. Winner(s) will be declared based on the scores given by the judges.
2. Winner(s) will be awarded with a trophy, medal, voucher and a cash prize.

Disqualification

1. Participants may be disqualified for violation of any event rules or misconduct.
2. Disqualified participants will forfeit any rights to awards.
3. Non-technical presentations will be out rightly rejected.

Liability

1. The event organizers and sponsors are not liable for any loss, injury, or damage incurred by participants during the event.
2. Participants are responsible for their own travel, accommodation, and other expenses related to Participation.

Agreement

1. By registering for the event, participants agree to abide by all event rules and decisions of the organizers.
2. Failure to comply with the rules may result in disqualification from the event.
3. The event organizers reserve the right to amend these rules at any time, with or without prior notice.
4. Any amendments will be communicated to participants through the official event channels.
